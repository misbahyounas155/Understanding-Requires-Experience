Does Understanding Require Experience?
Research Question

Can an artificial system truly understand something it has never experienced?

Modern AI can recognize patterns, generate language, and explain complex concepts with impressive accuracy. However, an important philosophical and cognitive science question remains:

Does producing the correct answer imply genuine understanding?
Background

John Searle's Chinese Room argument suggests that symbol manipulation alone may not constitute understanding.

Later, Varela, Thompson, and Rosch argued in The Embodied Mind that cognition emerges through interaction between the brain, body, and environment.

This raises an important possibility:

Understanding may require more than information.

It may require experience.
Key Ideas
Information is not necessarily understanding.
Memory provides continuity.
Experience gives context.
Embodiment may shape cognition.
AI currently demonstrates semantic knowledge rather than lived experience.
Current Reflection

If AI can explain pain without ever experiencing pain,

what exactly is missing?

Knowledge?

Experience?

Or something deeper?
